## STANDARDIZED COGNITIVE TRANSITION PATTERNS

After analyzing all 15 testimonies, here are the most prevalent cognitive transition patterns that create momentum and direction in thought:

### **Core Universal Patterns (Present in 10+ testimonies):**

1. **Recall-Redirection**: Interrupting current state by remembering a tool/technique
2. **Somatic-Anchoring**: Grounding awareness in physical sensations to shift mental state
3. **Externalization**: Moving thoughts from internal chaos to external structure (writing, mapping)
4. **Quantification-Scaling**: Rating/measuring subjective experience to gain perspective
5. **Pattern-Recognition**: Identifying the mechanism behind the experience
6. **Observer-Identification**: Separating the observing self from the experiencing self
7. **Temporal-Bounding**: Recognizing experiences have beginnings, middles, and ends
8. **Evidence-Checking**: Testing thoughts against actual evidence
9. **Reframe-Questioning**: Shifting from unproductive to productive questions
10. **Micro-Action**: Taking smallest possible step to build momentum

### **Secondary Patterns (Present in 5-9 testimonies):**

11. **Perspective-Inversion**: Looking at situation from opposite viewpoint
12. **Hypothesis-Testing**: Treating thoughts as theories to be tested
13. **Completion-Recognition**: Acknowledging small accomplishments to rebuild agency
14. **Value-Linking**: Connecting actions to deeper personal values
15. **Both-And-Thinking**: Moving from either/or to integration
16. **Scale-Adjustment**: Shifting between cosmic and human-sized meaning
17. **Narrative-Reconstruction**: Rewriting life story with different evidence selection
18. **Function-Recognition**: Understanding protective purpose of symptoms

### **Specialized Patterns (Context-specific but powerful):**

19. **Cognitive-Hijacking**: Using demanding mental tasks to break loops
20. **Reality-Affirmation**: Stating concrete facts about identity and location
21. **Future-Threading**: Connecting to specific future moments worth witnessing
22. **Protective-Reframe**: Understanding symptoms as nervous system protection
23. **Exit-Multiplication**: Finding alternative paths when primary route blocked
24. **Proof-of-Concept**: Using single success as evidence of possibility

These patterns represent the **functional mechanics** of cognitive state transitions - not what you think about, but HOW your thinking moves from stuck to flowing, from chaos to clarity, from overwhelm to agency. They are the momentum-builders and direction-changers of consciousness.
